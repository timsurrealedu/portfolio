# Product

## Register

brand

## Platform

web

## Users

Technical recruiters, engineering leads, security practitioners, and potential collaborators evaluating Timothy’s technical range, initiative, and ability to communicate. They need a fast path from personality to credible work and concrete responsibilities.

## Product Purpose

Present Timothy as a Cyber Security student who learns through real systems: building software, operating infrastructure, testing security ideas, and taking ownership in teams. Success means a visitor can identify his strongest work, understand how he thinks, and find a reason to start a conversation.

## Positioning

Security, systems, building, and leadership—demonstrated through work rather than claimed through titles.

## Conversion & proof

- Primary CTA: explore selected projects.
- Secondary CTA: visit GitHub and make contact.
- The line a visitor remembers after 10 seconds: “I build systems, secure software, and occasionally break my Linux installation.”
- Belief ladder: Timothy is curious → he ships and operates real systems → he understands security and infrastructure tradeoffs → he can own work and communicate it.
- Proof on hand: Sentinel, LifeOS, Stewie, TPM backend work, ADP program leadership, public speaking, and IELTS 8.0.

## Brand Personality

Mechanical, candid, and restless. Technically serious without hacker-theater; playful enough to admit that learning includes breaking things.

## Anti-references

Generic neon “cyberpunk” portfolios, green-on-black Matrix cosplay, inaccessible command-only navigation, rounded SaaS cards, fake telemetry, and a close visual copy of Winxen.

## Design Principles

- Make exploration optional, never mandatory: every command has a visible control.
- Show evidence before adjectives.
- Treat the terminal as a working environment, not a costume.
- Separate implemented work from concepts and plans.
- Let dense information remain calm through hierarchy and rhythm.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.2 AA, full keyboard access, visible focus, reduced-motion support, semantic landmarks, high-contrast text, and no information conveyed by color alone.
